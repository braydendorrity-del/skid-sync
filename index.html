§ 01 · The Problem

Harnesses Are
The Headache.

Different machines. Different connectors. Different harnesses. Big inventory headache and constant swap time on the job site — until now.

The Old Way

Wired Everything.

Harnesses routed through boom arms and cab glass get pinched, corroded, and ripped. "Will it work with my machine?" Nobody knows — until it doesn't.

  • — Machine-specific, brand-specific
  • — 30–60 min installs, every time
  • — Exposed connectors, field failures
  • — OEM kits: $6,000+
The Skid Sync Way

Wireless. Universal.

One battery-powered box. Any brand, any attachment. No cab wiring. Same cost as three harnesses — installs in minutes and survives the job site.

  • + Works with Bobcat, CAT, Deere, and more
  • + Epoxy-potted, sealed, fuse-protected

§ 03 · How It Works

Three Steps.
No Manual.

Mount the box. Connect the harness. Go to work. If you can plug in a trailer, you can install a Skid Sync.

01
Mount It

Clamp the U-400 or U-600 onto your attachment frame. No cab wiring, no drilling, no harness routing through boom arms.

Under 2 min
02
Connect It

Plug the attachment harness into the box. Deutsch, Delphi, DIN — we stock them all. Pair the remote with one button.

Under 1 min
03
Run It

Operate your attachment wirelessly from inside the cab. Swap to the next attachment in minutes — not an afternoon.

§ 06 · Our Story

Built In
Utah. Built For
The World.

Skid Sync was born from frustration on the job site. The options online were either junk or way too expensive — so we built what we wanted. Every box we ship is built, tested, and supported by the same people who run heavy equipment.

Operators were wasting hours fighting wiring harnesses that didn't fit, broke after a week, or required running cables through cab glass. We built a wireless control system that eliminates the problem entirely — mount it to the attachment, pair the remote, go to work.

We're a small team in Pleasant Grove, Utah. We come from equipment service — we've diagnosed the failures, we've dealt with the incompatible connectors. Everything we ship is built and tested by the same people who understand what it goes through in the field.
